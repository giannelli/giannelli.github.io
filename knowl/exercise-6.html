\(\lim_{t \to \infty} p(t)\)Answer\(p(\infty) = 1\)

After a long period of time everyone knows the rumour!

 Solution

\begin{align*} lim_{t \to \infty}\fe{p}{t}&=\frac{1}{1+ a e^{-k\infty}}\\ \text{Since }e^{-k\infty} = 0, \{ k \in \mathbb{R} | k \ge 0 \}\\ \therefore lim_{t \to \infty} p(t)&= \frac{1}{1+0}\\ &=1 \end{align*}
